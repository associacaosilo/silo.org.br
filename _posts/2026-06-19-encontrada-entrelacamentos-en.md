## **Proximity, collaboration, and assembly**

Since 2012, EncontrADA has occupied a singular place in the Brazilian feminist ecosystem. It is not a congress, not a training school, not a street mobilization. It is the space where women, trans and non-binary people from different generations, regions, and fields of action — activists, researchers, artists, educators, community leaders — come together to strengthen bonds, share knowledge, and develop collective strategies. Over seven editions, EncontrADA has generated connections that persist in the movements these people build throughout the year.

The 2026 edition, this eighth edition, proposes the concept of Entanglements: an invitation to strengthen connections between different feminisms, social movements, territories, ancestral knowledge, community practices, art, technology, and socio-environmental justice. EncontrADA 2026 will be a feminist assembly of collective intelligence, with participatory methodology oriented toward the production of shared visions of futures and concrete strategies to confront the violence advancing over bodies, territories, and rights.

## **What you will experience**

Over **four days**, Serrinha do Alambari hosts workshops, conversations, laboratories, communal living, and experiences built from the exchange of different knowledge and practices.

The programming open to the public includes activities and workshops spanning themes such as home herbal medicine, natural gynecology, ceramics, audiovisual, theater, dance, technology, citizen data generation and accessibility, as well as a cultural program.

One of the central axes is dedicated to masculinities, aimed at boys and men — a space for reflection centered on life and care.

In this edition, we will once again hold the **Confabulations**, which bring together leaders from feminist organizations and movements from different regions of the country to think about the future of organizations. The theme of this edition is masculinities: how does feminism name, challenge, and transform the models of masculinity it wants to see in the world? The question that will guide the axis is: **how do we set the agenda for the masculinity we imagine?**

Feminist organizations need to actively deal with what men do with the power they hold — and with the futures they imagine for themselves. Drawing from the experiences and reflections brought by participants, the Confabulations will produce a **message manual on future-centered, care-oriented masculinities**, for use by Brazilian feminist organizations.

The full program, with workshops, facilitators, and guests, will be announced soon.

## **Location and context**

Silo - Art and Rural Latitude is located in Serrinha do Alambari, which is an integral part of an Environmental Protection Area (APA), situated in the municipality of Resende (RJ), on the eastern slope of the Itatiaia National Park, Serra da Mantiqueira mountain range. This area has the particularity of integrating rural zones and environmental protection zones.

## **Safeguarding Policy and Code of Ethics**

Silo, as an organization formed and self-managed by women, prioritizes listening and mutual respect in the creation of a pleasant and healthy work environment permeated by care. Therefore, any discriminatory behavior, whether based on color, origin, ethnicity, age, gender, sexual orientation, religious beliefs, political-party or union affiliation, marital status, or any other type against the dignity of the person, is not tolerated. Selected participants agree, at the time of registration, to read and adhere to Silo - Art and Rural Latitude's Safeguarding Policy and Code of Ethics and Conduct, committing to adopt the commitments established in those documents throughout the entire formative process.

## **History**

<a href="https://silo.org.br/encontrada/" target="_blank">EncontrADA</a> is a feminist gathering focused on the exchange of knowledge between different feminist perspectives that make up distinct modes of existence. It began in 2012 through the open call "Body, feminism, and technology: free!", with the aim of establishing a network to provoke the importance of technologies in feminism. From the first EncontrADA onward, the immersion became a place for intergenerational gathering of different feminist groups interested in crafts, technologies, and ancestry.
